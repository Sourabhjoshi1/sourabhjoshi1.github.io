Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of power electronics, energy storage, and control, with a particular focus on how these systems evolve over long time horizons and shape future technological infrastructure.

Primary Interests

  • DC–DC converters and high-efficiency power electronic topologies
  • Bidirectional converters for battery energy storage systems
  • Battery Management Systems (BMS)
  • State-of-Charge (SoC) and State-of-Health (SoH) estimation
  • Control of power electronic and energy systems

Current Directions

I am currently exploring high-efficiency converter design for battery applications, with emphasis on realistic operating conditions, long-term degradation, and system-level trade-offs rather than idealized performance metrics.

In parallel, I am interested in the use of machine learning and data-driven methods for battery modeling, particularly where traditional physics-based models struggle to capture complex aging phenomena.

Broader Questions

Beyond immediate technical problems, I am interested in broader questions such as:

  • How should energy systems be designed when evaluated over decades rather than years?
  • What are the limits of efficiency, reliability, and control in large-scale energy storage?
  • How can engineering remain grounded in physical reality while adopting data-driven methods?
